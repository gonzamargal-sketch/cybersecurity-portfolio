# Internal Security Audit — Botium Toys

## Overview

This directory contains the artifacts of an internal IT security audit conducted for **Botium Toys**, a fictional small U.S. toy manufacturer and retailer used as a case study.

The audit was carried out following the **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (NIST CSF)** and covers the review of existing controls, identification of compliance gaps, and an assessment of risks to the company's assets and operations.

## Business Context

Botium Toys operates from a single physical location that serves as office, storefront, and warehouse. Its online presence has grown significantly, now reaching customers both domestically and internationally — including the European Union.

This growth introduced new pressure on the IT department to:

- Secure an expanding digital infrastructure
- Comply with regulations around online payments (e.g., PCI DSS)
- Meet EU data protection requirements (e.g., GDPR)

## Audit Goals

The IT manager initiated the audit to answer a core question: *Is the company's current security posture adequate to support continued growth while managing risk and staying compliant?*

Specific goals include:

1. Identify and document assets managed by the IT department
2. Assess current risks, threats, and vulnerabilities
3. Evaluate existing security controls against best practices
4. Check compliance against relevant regulatory frameworks
5. Provide a prioritized set of findings and recommendations

## Methodology

The audit follows a structured approach:

1. **Scope and goals definition** — established by the IT manager
2. **Asset inventory** — cataloguing what the IT department manages
3. **Risk assessment** — scoring likelihood and impact of identified risks
4. **Controls and compliance checklist** — evaluating whether required controls are in place
